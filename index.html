Dwin

MTOTO WA Dodoma, Formerly of Dxquad

ABOUT

Dwin is an East African artist, writer, rapper, and musician who emerged from Dodoma City’s underground rap scene. Starting his journey in local cyphers, he quickly built a name for himself with his sharp lyricism and unique delivery. His breakthrough came with the hit track Serikali kuu, alongside Young Lunya, Moni Centralzone, Countrywizzy and producer Paul Maker, which became a defining moment in the Country's rap scene. Dwin’s feature on Rapcha’s Unaua Vibe alongside Young Lunya further elevated his status, as the song became one of the most celebrated rap records of the year. His role in Maelekezo Chapter One, the first installment of Moni Centralzone’s iconic series, solidified his position as a rising star and paved the way for future collaborations.

Discography

Dwin’s solo debut, Niongezee, showcased his artistic versatility by blending experimental sounds with African Bongo Fleva hip-hop. The song received widespread acclaim for its innovation, cementing his reputation as a trailblazer in East African music. Following his groundbreaking solo debut, Niongezee, Dwin continued to showcase his versatility with Mara Ya Mwisho, a deeply emotional love song that struck a chord with fans. He then unleashed Muda wangu, a powerful anthem that not only trended on YouTube but also secured spots on Africa’s most influential hip-hop playlists. Building on this momentum, Dwin dropped his highly anticipated EP, 270—a tribute to the street that shaped his journey. Featuring three standout tracks—Simba, Pray, and This side—the project blended raw storytelling with diverse soundscapes, drawing in new fans and reaffirming Dwin as a visionary force in East African rap. 270 wasn’t just music; it was a movement, a reflection of where he came from and where he’s headed.
